Line clear types

  • Single: 1 line is cleared at once

  • Double: 2 lines are cleared at once

  • Triple: 3 lines are cleared at once. This can be done with normal L/J clear or with S/Z spin clear.

  • Tetris/Quad: 4 lines are cleared simultaniously. This can only be done with the I piece.

Garbage

Garbage is the main form of attack in Tetris multiplayer. Basically, how it works is if you clear multiple rows or ‘lines’ at once, garbage lines will be added to the bottom of your opponent’s board with a single gap. The more lines you clear, the more garbage it will send.

4-5 Combo 1,2,3,4 +2 (and then +1 for each additional two combos)

This table applies to the TETR.IO system. Other Tetris games might be using a different system to this one.

Cancelling garbage

To cancel oncoming garbage, the player will have to send the same number of lines as the garbage. E.g. if 6 lines of garbage were sent to you, you must clear 6 or more lines in return to cancel it out. Clearing more than that will send lines back towards the opponent.

The bar on the right or left side of the board is the oncoming garbage. The height of that bar corresponds to how high the garbage being sent to you will go. For example, the image shows the bar going up 5 lines, this means you are being sent 5 lines of garbage.

Combo

A combo is when you make a line clear with one piece and then quickly follow with another line clear before a timer ends. A continuous combo adds to the number of lines a player sends. Look to the garbage table for specifics.

Back-to-Back (B2B)

A back-to-back or B2B is when a player clears only Tetrises or t-spins consecutively without any other type of line clearing in between. A B2B chain increases every time a quad or t-spin is cleared, once any other type of clear is made the chain will break and you will have to restart the chain.
