7-bag Randomizer

In Tetris, the queue is made using the 7-bag randomizer method. Every 7 pieces (L,J,S,Z,I,O,T) in the queue will be the 7 tetrominos in a random order, this is so that the order and frequency of your pieces in the queue will be fair and consistent.

Hold

The hold feature lets the player store the current piece and swaps it with what was in hold previously, or with the next piece in queue if there is nothing in hold. Useful for keeping a piece for later use. Utilizing the hold feature is an essential skill to be able to pull off openers swiftly. Its most commonly used to hold a T piece for a t-spin later on, or to build a certain setup which requires a specific order of pieces.

Perfect Clear (PC)

A PC is when the player achieves a perfectly empty board. This is a special type of line clear that is rewarded.

T-spins

>>

A t-spin is created by placing the T piece in a position where it’s surrounded by other blocks on 3 sides. Then you spin the t-piece either clockwise or counterclockwise.

This will make the t-piece “spin”. If done correctly the t-piece will clear at least 2 lines, and possibly more. A normal t-spin will send 4 lines despite only clearing 2 lines, this is because t-spins in particular are rewarded in tetris. They will also be counted in a back-to-back streak.

There are other variations of t-spins such as the t-spin single, t-spin triple, and t-spin mini.

SDPC Opener

There are maybe hundreds of effective openers which can be played in tetris that all serve different purposes, whether to open with a spin, continue into an extended opener, or to set up for stacking. But one that will be covered in this page is called the SDPC. The SDPC opener has many variations, but starting is always the same.

To successfully build the SDPC opener, it’s necessary to have an early O piece, particularly before the S and T piece as both of these cannot be placed until the O piece is in the board. If only one of them comes before the O piece, then that is fine because you can just store it in hold until the O piece is down.

This is the starting of the SDPC opener which uses all 7 pieces of the first bag. It sets up a t-spin which is used to build the 2nd step of the SDPC opener.

T-spin triple

While there are many ways to continue the SDPC opener from here, a great way to follow a t-spin is with another t-spin! Specifically, the t-spin triple as it will send 6 lines despite only clearing 3.

Doing this right after the first t-spin will start a combo, which if played swiftly will send at least 10 lines to the enemy, which is basically half the board!


After clearing the first t-spin, we move on to the 2nd bag of SDPC. Clear the t-spin, then to the 3rd bag which sets up a t-spin triple into a t-spin double.

Other Openers

Having an armory of different openers is essential if you want to pull off a lethal entrance in your next vs matches. These two previously explained openers are great to use when queueing into a multiplayer match, Although normal stacking can also come in clutch in times when it is more difficult to foresee a possible opener. For this reason, it is ideal to have multiple different openers even different from the ones previously mentioned.

There are many MANY other openers out there which haven’t been covered in this page, all of which play a different purpose depending on how a player wants to play. It is recommended to check out more opener varieties to be able to build a decent armory of openers to use against your next opponent.
